Retrieval architecture

Hybrid retrieval pipeline for Azerbaijani legal QA

The first iteration of E-Huquq used dense retrieval alone. It was fluent and wrong. Legal queries hinge on exact terms (article numbers, statute names) that semantic retrieval will happily paraphrase away.

The fix was a parallel sparse index over the same corpus and a fusion step that preserved both signals. Reciprocal Rank Fusion gave a robust default; an LLM reranking pass on the top candidates closed the gap between “a good document is in the top-50” and “a good document is at rank 1.”

The lesson, restated: in domains where vocabulary is load-bearing, sparse retrieval is not legacy.

NLU + graph

Metro Bot — route planning over a station graph

Pure ML overkill for a problem that’s mostly graph traversal. The Baku Metro is a small graph; BFS gives optimal routes. The interesting work was upstream: resolving misspelled station names from English/Azerbaijani transliterations, handling colloquial nicknames, and producing step-wise directions that read like a person wrote them.

NLP for low-resource Azerbaijani

Spelling correction with LSTM + attention

Azerbaijani spelling errors in social-media text follow predictable patterns — vowel substitutions, missing diacritics, suffix collapse. A Seq2Seq LSTM with attention, trained on 92,500 sentence pairs, reached 97.4% accuracy and outperformed off-the-shelf correction tools that don’t handle the morphology well.

Published as Integrating Attention Mechanisms with LSTM for Azerbaijani Spelling Correction at ICMSO 2024.
