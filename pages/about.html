About

How I think about AI systems

The interesting problem in applied AI is rarely the model. It’s everything wrapped around it — data pipelines, retrieval, evaluation, the failure modes nobody warned you about.

I’m an AI Engineer at MegaSec AI Laboratory and a Researcher at the Center of Data Analytics and Research at ADA University. I split my time between shipping legal AI systems that go into ministries and studying the methods that make those systems hold up under real query distributions.

Most of my work involves Azerbaijani — a language with limited NLP resources, which forces a different set of tradeoffs than the ones English-language AI literature usually optimises for. That constraint has been one of the more productive ones in my career.

What I work on

  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation — hybrid dense and sparse indexes, fusion strategies, LLM reranking. The kind of RAG that survives a domain shift.
  • Legal AI — building retrieval and grounded answer systems for legislative corpora, starting with Azerbaijani law.
  • Multi-agent architectures — intent routing, specialist agents, evaluation frameworks for agent quality.
  • Low-resource Azerbaijani NLP — embeddings, spelling correction, sentiment analysis.

How I work

I write the evaluation set first. If I can’t define what good retrieval looks like for a domain before I touch a model, the project is going to drift. Once the eval is in place, the rest is mostly measurement.

I prefer simple architectures that I can debug at 2am over clever ones I can’t. Production systems decay; the code that runs longest is the code I can read six months later.

Research and engineering aren’t separate tracks for me. The papers I’ve published came out of problems I hit while shipping — and the systems I’ve shipped have benefited from methods I had to read up on first.
