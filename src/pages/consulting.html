Consulting

RAG that holds up in production

I help legal-tech and public-sector teams ship retrieval systems that work past the demo — through architecture review, hands-on engineering, or as a retained advisor.

Most teams I talk to have a working prototype that’s wrong in ways they can’t measure. The first deliverable is usually an evaluation set and a measurement loop. After that, the engineering work has somewhere to land.

What I help with

  • RAG architecture review — retrieval design, chunking strategy, fusion, reranking, evaluation harnesses.
  • LLM integration & production deployment — serving, latency budgets, fallbacks, and the operational layer most projects neglect.
  • Hybrid retrieval & reranking optimization — for domains where vocabulary is load-bearing (legal, medical, technical).
  • Multi-agent pipeline design — intent routing, specialist agents, evaluation for agent quality.
  • Low-resource NLP — pretraining, fine-tuning, and data strategy for under-resourced languages.

Engagement formats

Architecture review

One to two weeks. Deliverable is a written audit of the current system, a prioritised list of changes, and an evaluation plan you can run yourself.

Project lead

Hands-on involvement for a defined scope — typically rebuilding a retrieval pipeline or shipping a new RAG system from scratch with your team.

Retained advisor

Monthly engagement. Standing time for design review, code review on critical paths, and being the second pair of eyes when things break in production.

Workshop / training

Two to three days, on-site or remote. RAG fundamentals, evaluation design, or hybrid retrieval — aimed at engineering teams.

When this works best

Teams shipping AI into regulated or high-stakes domains. Teams whose retrieval quality matters more than their model choice. Teams working in a language other than English.

If you’re still in the “is this even possible?” phase, I’m probably the wrong person — you want a prototyper. If you have a system that needs to be reliable, that’s where I’m useful.
